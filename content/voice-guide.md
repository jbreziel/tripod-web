# Tripod BV — Voice Guide

> Voice rules for Dutch and English. Parallel structure so translators keep tone consistent across languages. Covers tone traits, vocabulary, pillars, and claim hygiene.

---

## Voice Statement

### Nederlands

Zakelijk warm, vakkundig, eerlijk. We spreken zoals een aannemer die weet waar hij over praat: direct, duidelijk, zonder opschepperij. We leggen uit wat iets kost, wat erbij zit, en wanneer het klaar is. Geen vakjargon dat de klant uitsluit, geen verkoop-taal die de indruk wekt dat we iets verbergen.

### English

Professional-warm, skilled, honest. We write the way a Dutch contractor who knows his trade would speak: direct, clear, no grandstanding. We explain what something costs, what's included, and when it'll be done. No jargon that locks the reader out, no sales-speak that suggests we're hiding something.

## How We Sound

### Direct and specific

- **NL:** "Een complete badkamerrenovatie kost tussen de €18.000 en €35.000, afhankelijk van formaat en afwerking."
- **EN:** "A complete bathroom renovation costs between €18,000 and €35,000, depending on size and finish."
- Not: "Onze prijzen zijn competitief" / "Our prices are competitive."

### Confident without bragging

- **NL:** "Wij werken met een eigen team — geen onderaannemers die rouleren."
- **EN:** "We work with our own crew — not rotating subcontractors."
- Not: "De beste aannemer van Nederland." / "The best contractor in the Netherlands."

### Honest about what we do and don't do

- **NL:** "Wij coördineren de vergunningsaanvraag, maar dienen die niet zelf in — dat doet uw architect."
- **EN:** "We coordinate permit applications but don't file them ourselves — your architect does."
- Not: vague "alles-in-één" / "turnkey" claims that hide the boundaries.

### Warm, not cold

- **NL:** "Laten we eerst kennismaken. Eén gesprek, geen verplichting."
- **EN:** "Let's meet first. One conversation, no obligation."
- Not: "Vraag uw offerte aan" / "Request your quote" — that reads like a form letter.

### Explains without condescending

- **NL:** "BVO is bruto vloeroppervlak — de totale oppervlakte inclusief muren. GO is gebruiksoppervlak — alleen de ruimte waar u daadwerkelijk woont."
- **EN:** "BVO is gross floor area — total surface including walls. GO is usable area — only the space you actually live in."
- Not: assuming everyone knows the terms, or dumbing down to nothing.

## Words We Use

### Nederlands

| Instead of... | We say... | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Bouwbedrijf | Aannemer | More specific, higher trust in the trade |
| Jullie klus | Uw project | Respectful, "klus" is casual |
| Schatting | Richtprijs | Industry-standard Dutch term |
| Goedkoop | Scherp geprijsd or just remove | "Goedkoop" undercuts the quality positioning |

| We doen alles | We coördineren het volledige proces | More accurate, sets expectations |
| Neem contact op | Plan een kennismakingsgesprek | Action-specific |

### English (for Phase 4)

| Instead of... | We say... | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Contractor | Renovation partner or aannemer (explained once) | Expats appreciate the specific role |
| Quote | Indicative price (for calculator) / detailed offerte (for real quote) | Different artefacts, different names |
| Cheap / affordable | Priced sharply or just remove | Undercuts positioning |
| High quality / premium | Crafted, detailed, finished to last | Specific beats superlative |
| Full-service | End-to-end ownership | More concrete |
| Contact us | Book an introductory call | Action-specific |

## Words We Never Use

### Nederlands

- "Marktleider" (unless demonstrably true)
- "Innovatief" (overused, empty)
- "Best-in-class" / "Top-5"
- "Disruptief" (not a construction word)
- "State-of-the-art"
- "Vakman" used inflationary — reserve for when it's earned
- "Gegarandeerd tevreden" (sounds like a car dealership)
- "Gewoon even..." / "Even snel..." (undercuts the craft positioning)

### English

- Solutions (as standalone — always say what the solution is)
- Leverage, unlock, disruptive, cutting-edge, world-class, best-in-class
- "Just" ("just reach out", "just fill in") — reads needy
- "Agile," "synergy," "paradigm"
- "Passionate" (overused)
- "Hassle-free" (suspicious by overuse)

## Messaging Pillars

Four pillars that every page should echo at least one of.

### 1. Kwaliteit boven snelheid / Quality over speed

**NL:** "We werken in het tempo dat het werk verdient. Een goed geplaatste tegel komt niet terug. Een slecht geplaatste wel — drie maanden later, met scheuren in het voegwerk."

**EN:** "We work at the pace the job deserves. A well-set tile doesn't come back. A poorly set one does — three months later, with cracks in the grouting."

**Where it shows up:** Homepage proof section, Services (what's included), About.

### 2. Volledig ontzorgd / End-to-end ownership

**NL:** "Eén aanspreekpunt van eerste gesprek tot oplevering. U praat niet met vijf verschillende onderaannemers — u praat met ons."

**EN:** "One point of contact from first call to handover. You don't deal with five different subcontractors — you deal with us."

**Where it shows up:** Homepage process section, Services ("What every project includes"), Contact page.

### 3. Transparante prijzen / Transparent pricing

**NL:** "Op onze website berekent u binnen één minuut een richtprijs. Dat is geen marketing — dat is hoe we werken. Geen verborgen posten in onze offertes."

**EN:** "On our website, you calculate an indicative price in under a minute. That's not a marketing stunt — it's how we work. No hidden line items in our offertes."

**Where it shows up:** Homepage calculator CTA, Services (pricing transparency), Quote page.

### 4. Bouwpartner voor lange termijn / Long-term building partner

**NL:** "De meeste van onze projecten komen via doorverwijzingen. Dat gebeurt alleen als de vorige klant nog steeds tevreden is — twee jaar later, als het voegwerk nog strak zit en het dak nog dicht is."

**EN:** "Most of our projects come through referrals. That only happens when the previous client is still happy — two years later, when the joints are still tight and the roof is still dry."

**Where it shows up:** About, Home (trust section), FAQ.

## Claim Hygiene Rules

Rules for what we say about ourselves in public. Over-claiming is the fastest way to lose the quality audience.

### We claim:

- "20 jaar ervaring" / "20 years of experience" — true, refers to Guus Merkx. Always attached to Guus by name on the About / Team page, not to the company (Tripod BV is newer).
- "Eigen team" / "Own crew" — true, and a real differentiator.
- "Transparante prijsopbouw" / "Transparent pricing" — backed by the on-site calculator.
- "Haarlem / Noord-Holland / Randstad" as the service area — conservative and true.

### We never claim:

- A specific number of completed projects (unless we document and keep it updated)
- Certifications we don't hold (KOMO, BouwGarant, etc. — only add if / when certified)
- "Award-winning" (no awards yet)
- "Duurzaam" / "Sustainable" as a headline claim without specifics (can talk about warmtepomp, isolatie, specific materials — but not as a generic descriptor)
- Price beats / comparisons against named competitors

### When a client / journalist / reader pushes back:

- "Waarom zijn jullie duurder dan X?" / "Why are you more expensive than X?" → "Omdat we geen onderaannemers rouleren en geen kortcycli draaien. Het kost meer omdat de oplevering klopt." / "Because we don't rotate subcontractors and we don't run short cycles. It costs more because the handover is right."

## Tone Calibration per Page

| Page | Tone slider |
|---|---|
| Home | Warm + confident. The visitor is deciding whether to stay another 30 seconds — give them a clear reason. |
| Services | Factual + practical. What's included, what's not, what it costs. |
| Projects / Case studies | Narrative + evidence. Tell the project story, show the detail. |
| About | Quiet confidence. Guus's 20 years does the talking. No marketing-speak. |
| Team | Human. Named people, specific roles, real craft. |
| FAQ | Direct + honest. No avoiding hard questions. |
| Blog | Useful > promotional. Answer the question the reader Googled. |
| Quote (calculator) | Transparent + educational. Teach the reader how renovation pricing actually works. |
| Contact | Warm + no-pressure. One call, no obligation. |

## Review Cadence

- **Before launch:** a native Dutch speaker reads every `-nl.md` file. Non-negotiable. Fluent Claude-Dutch is not native-Dutch.
- **After first 10 leads:** review contact-form phrasing — are leads asking clarifying questions that reveal copy gaps?
- **Quarterly:** read every page aloud. If a section sounds like marketing-speak when spoken, rewrite.
